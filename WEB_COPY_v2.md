### 2.2 The problem (reframed: metrics are useful, but…)

**Eyebrow**
> PACE IS DATA. THE RUN IS A STORY.

**Headline (recommended)**
> Numbers helped you train. They never helped you remember.

_Alt:_
> _Your watch knows the pace. It doesn't know the place._
> _A run is more than the number you finish it with._

**Body**
> Pace, splits, heart-rate zones — they have their place. They tell you
> when you're getting fitter, when you went too hard, how you are improving over time.
> Thats all useful.
>
> But somewhere along the way, the numbers started overshadowing the run
> itself. Your view of one legged Egret meditating on the lake at km 4. Sunlight dancing through the trees on a crisp sunday morning.
> The friend who ran beside you. The reason you laced up at
> all. None of that fits into a chart

**Pull quote (callout, set in serif / large)**
> "Pace is a number. The run is a memory."

### 2.4 A different philosophy (quote stack — kept, lightly reframed)

**Eyebrow**
> A DIFFERENT PHILOSOPHY

**Lead**
> ZenRun stands on shoulders of runners and writers who already knew
> something the fitness industry forgot.

**Quote 1 — Christopher McDougall, _Born to Run_** (coral accent)
> "Sometimes, we complicate things with gadgets and gear, when what we
> really need is to trust our bodies and keep things simple."
>
> The Tarahumara run hundreds of miles in thin sandals, smiling. No GPS.
> No heart-rate monitors. They run because running is woven into the
> fabric of how they live — a form of community, prayer, and celebration.

**Quote 2 — Haruki Murakami, _What I Talk About When I Talk About Running_** (teal accent)
> "The only opponent you have to beat is yourself, the way you used to be."
>
> Murakami has run marathons for four decades. He doesn't run to live
> longer. He runs to live fully. What matters is whether you improved
> over yesterday — not where you rank on a leaderboard.

**Quote 3 — George Sheehan, _Running & Being_** (neutral)
> "It is only necessary that he runs and runs and sometimes suffers.
> Then one day he will wake up and discover that somewhere along the way
> he has begun to see order and law and love and Truth."
>
> Running transforms the runner. Not through data, but through the
> accumulated experience of showing up, day after day.

### 2.5 The album is the difference

> _New section. Earns its place because it's the most original idea on
> the page — and the v1 site never named it directly._

**Eyebrow**
> THE ALBUM

**Headline (recommended)**
> The numbers stay. The memories get a place to live.

_Alt:_
> _Two halves of one run: the pace, and the place._

**Body**

>

### 2.8 Pillar — Show up (the path)

**Eyebrow**
> THE PATH

**Headline**
> Show up.

**Lead paragraph (lightly softened)**
> The shoes-on, out-the-door part of running. ZenRun stays out of your
> way: Run with your phone as you take pictures; the watch app, if you use it, is a
> quiet companion; the data lives in the journal, not in your face. Show
> up enough times and you stop calling yourself someone who runs
> sometimes — you become a runner.

**Feature tiles (6)** — unchanged from v1, with one suggested edit:

1. 🗺️ The photo run — GPS from your phone or wrist. Stop on the bridge, take the photo, keep going. ZenRun drops it on your route at the right km, and the album builds itself.
2. 🌳 **Weekly rhythm** — Two runs (or walks) a week keeps your rhythm going. Not pace. Not distance. Just presence.
3. ⌚ **Quiet Apple Watch companion** — One tap to start. GPS captured in the background. The wrist stays calm so the run stays the run.
4. 🚶 **Walks count too** — Walks are first-class. Same logging, same rhythm, same scenic photos. Show up however you can.
5. 🎯 **Goals you can keep** — Yearly and monthly km targets. A simple bar shows where you are, where you should be, and nothing more.
6. 🎉 **Quiet celebrations** — A small moment of confetti when you keep your rhythm or hit a goal. No fanfare. Just a nod.
